Early Computing

Mechanical Roots

Before electronic computers, people built mechanical devices to assist calculation. Notable examples include the abacus, Pascal's calculator (1642), and Charles Babbage's designs for the Difference Engine and Analytical Engine (1830s).

Ada Lovelace

Working with Babbage, Ada Lovelace wrote notes that described how the Analytical Engine could compute Bernoulli numbers. These notes are often considered the first published computer program (1843).

From Theory to Machines

Alan Turing's 1936 paper introduced the idea of a universal machine, shaping the theory of computation. In the 1940s, electronic, programmable machines such as ENIAC and Colossus brought computing into the electronic era.
