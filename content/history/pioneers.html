Pioneers of Programming

Ada Lovelace

Wrote what is often considered the first published computer program in 1843 for Babbage's Analytical Engine.

Alan Turing

Formulated the concept of a universal machine and contributed to codebreaking and early computing.

Grace Hopper

Pioneered compilers and helped create COBOL, promoting the idea that programming languages can be human-readable.

John Backus

Led the team that created Fortran and introduced BNF notation for describing language grammars.

Margaret Hamilton

Led the software engineering effort for NASA's Apollo missions, helping land humans on the Moon.
